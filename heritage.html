Heritage Sites in Kottayam

Kottayam has a rich heritage and is home to several historic places that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top heritage places to visit in Kottayam:

  • Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple
    •

    Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple is one of the oldest temples in Kottayam and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It was built in the 16th century and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings. The temple has a gopuram, or tower, which is adorned with intricate sculptures and carvings. Inside the temple, there is a sanctum sanctorum where the main deity, Lord Shiva, is enshrined. The temple also has several other shrines dedicated to various other deities. The temple is known for its annual festival, Thirunakkara Arattu, which attracts thousands of devotees from all over Kerala.

  • St. Mary's Orthodox Syrian Church
    •

    St. Mary's Orthodox Syrian Church is one of the oldest churches in India and is located in Kottayam. The church was built in the 5th century and is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful paintings. The church has a wooden ceiling and is adorned with beautiful paintings that depict scenes from the Bible. The church also has a museum that houses several artifacts, including ancient manuscripts, old coins, and other religious artifacts. The church attracts thousands of tourists and devotees every year, especially during the annual festival, which is celebrated in September.

  • Thazhathangady Juma Masjid
    •

    The Thazhathangady Juma Masjid is an ancient mosque located in Kottayam. It was built in the traditional Kerala style of architecture and is known for its beautiful wooden carvings and intricate designs. The mosque has a central courtyard and is adorned with several arches and pillars. The mosque also has a large prayer hall where the faithful gather for daily prayers. The mosque is located on the banks of the Meenachil River and is a popular destination for tourists and history buffs.

  • Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple
    •

    The Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple is a famous temple located about 12 kilometers from Kottayam. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its stunning murals and beautiful architecture. The temple has a gopuram, or tower, which is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple is known for its stunning murals that depict scenes from the Hindu epic, Ramayana. The temple is also famous for its annual festival, Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple Festival, which is celebrated in February and March.

  • Vazhappally Maha Siva Temple
    •

    The Vazhappally Maha Siva Temple is a famous temple located about 7 kilometers from Kottayam. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its stunning murals and beautiful architecture. The temple has a gopuram, or tower, which is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple is known for its stunning murals that depict scenes from the Hindu epic, Mahabharata. The temple is also famous for its annual festival, Vazhappally Temple Festival, which is celebrated in February and March.

  • Poonjar Palace
    •

    Poonjar Palace is a historic palace located about 45 kilometers from Kottayam. It was built in the 16th century and is known for its beautiful architecture, including wooden carvings and intricate paintings. The palace was the residence of the Poonjar royal family and is now a museum that showcases several artifacts, including weapons, ancient manuscripts, and other relics. The palace also has a large courtyard and a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

  • Cheriyapally
    •

    Cheriyapally is a historic church located in the heart of Kottayam town. It was built in the 1570s by Portuguese missionaries and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings. The church has a blend of Gothic and Portuguese styles, and it is one of the oldest churches in Kottayam. The church has a grand entrance with a beautiful rose window above the main door. The interior of the church is adorned with beautiful paintings, statues, and intricate carvings. The church is a popular tourist attraction and is visited by people of all faiths.

  • Thiruvanchoor Mahadeva Temple
    •

    The Thiruvanchoor Mahadeva Temple is a famous temple located about 5 kilometers from Kottayam. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful murals. The temple has a unique Dravidian-style architecture, and it is believed to have been constructed in the 16th century. The temple has a tall gopuram (entrance tower) with intricate carvings and sculptures. The inner sanctum of the temple houses a beautiful Shiva Lingam, and the walls of the temple are adorned with colorful murals depicting various mythological stories.

  • Thekkumkoor Palace
    •

    The Thekkumkoor Palace is a historic palace located in the town of Pala, about 30 kilometers from Kottayam. It was the residence of the Thekkumkoor dynasty and is known for its beautiful architecture and well-preserved interiors. The palace has a blend of traditional Kerala and European styles, and it has a grand entrance with a beautiful courtyard. The palace has several rooms with intricate carvings and paintings, and it houses a museum that showcases various artifacts and relics from the region's rich history. The palace is a popular destination for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

These are just a few of the many heritage sites that Kottayam has to offer. Be sure to plan a visit to some of these fascinating places during your stay in the city!
