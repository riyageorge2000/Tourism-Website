About Kottayam

Kottayam is a city that is rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. It is located in the midland region of the state of Kerala, in the southwestern part of India. The city is situated on the banks of the Meenachil River and is surrounded by the Western Ghats mountain range. The city covers an area of about 55 square kilometers and has a population of around 136,000 people. Kottayam is well-known for its backwaters, which are a network of interconnected canals, lagoons, and lakes that are a unique feature of Kerala. The backwaters in Kottayam are particularly scenic, with lush greenery, coconut palms, and paddy fields lining the banks of the waterways. The backwaters are a popular tourist attraction and are often used for leisurely boat rides and houseboat cruises. Apart from the backwaters, Kottayam is also known for its many historic churches and temples. The St. Mary's Orthodox Syrian Church, which is located in the heart of the city, is one of the oldest churches in India. The Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, is another popular tourist attraction. The city is also home to several prestigious educational institutions. The Mahatma Gandhi University, which is located in Kottayam, is one of the most prestigious universities in Kerala. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various fields, including science, technology, arts, and humanities. The CMS College, which is one of the oldest colleges in India, is also located in Kottayam. The college was established in 1817 and offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various fields, including commerce, science, and humanities. Other popular educational institutions in Kottayam include the Baselius College, the Bishop Speechly College for Advanced Studies, and the St. Thomas College. Kottayam is also known for its thriving publishing industry, with many leading newspapers and magazines based in the city. The Malayala Manorama, which is one of the largest circulated newspapers in India, has its headquarters in Kottayam. The city is also home to other popular newspapers and magazines, including the Deepika, Mathrubhumi, and Vanitha. Apart from the backwaters, churches, temples, educational institutions, and publishing industry, Kottayam is also known for its cuisine. The local cuisine is a blend of traditional Kerala cuisine and Christian and Syrian Jewish influences. Some of the popular dishes in Kottayam include appam and stew, puttu and kadala curry, fish curry, and kappa and meen curry. In conclusion, Kottayam is a vibrant and culturally rich city that offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity. The city's rich history, natural beauty, and excellent educational institutions make it a popular destination for tourists and students alike. Whether you want to explore the backwaters, visit historic churches and temples, or enjoy the local cuisine, Kottayam has something for everyone. So, if you are planning a trip to Kerala, make sure to add Kottayam to your itinerary.
